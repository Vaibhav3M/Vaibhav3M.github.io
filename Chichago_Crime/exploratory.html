Exploratory Analysis

Data exploration helps infer useful information and predict important aspects of crime detection and prevention. At the same time, the nature of the data largely determines what kind of prediction tasks can be performed to identify useful features for building a predictive model. It revealed some interesting trends and patterns which are documented below.

Timelapse of criminal hot stops in Chicago city:

 

Criminal activity across the years 2010 to 2019 is plotted. We can see that crime along with the port increases across the years. Also, in most of some areas criminal activity is constant.

Distribution of Crime types(Before data preprocessing):

We can see that theft has occurred most frequently as it makes up of 22% of crime. Theft, battery, criminal damage, and narcotics are the most frequent occur crimes in Chicago which make up of 65.7% out of all the crimes. This means there is a high imbalance in dataset, we will combine som types and drop some irrelevant ones.

Distribution of Crime types(After merging and dropping):

 

Crime types have to reduce to 16. We have somewhat more balanced data that before but we will implement sampling techniques for further balancing.

Locations with frequent crimes(top 15):

We can see that street and sidewalk constitute the highest crimes. The reason could be that it is easier to get away if a person is familiar to area.

Crime is also high in personal spaces such as residence and apartment.

The trend of crime types across the years:

Crime rates, in general is decreasing, across the years other than the year 2016, where it sore for some types. There is a major decrease in Trespass, this could be due to improved security infrastructure.

Number arrests corresponding to the crimes changed over time:

We can see that there is an overall reduction in crime. The comparison between the arrest and crimes amount for each year let us see how the police abilities were not quite great since none of the arrests were higher than half of the crime rate.
